ReadLite · Quick Guide

3-Minute Onboarding to Immersive Reading

Styled the same as the reader so what you see here matches the real reading experience.

1. Enter Reading Mode

  1. Pin ReadLite to your browser toolbar from the extensions panel.
  2. Open any article and click the ReadLite toolbar icon to enter reading mode.
  3. A floating control bar appears; collapse it or close the reader anytime.

2. Control Bar at a Glance

  • Themes: 6 built-in themes, instant preview.
  • Fonts: pick any (includes monospace options), applied to body and code.
  • Size / Line height: slider adjustments, live updates.
  • Width: 30 / 50 / 70 / 100 steps for different screens and postures.
  • Alignment: left or justify.
  • Close: “×” at top-right exits reading mode.

3. Themes & Layout

  • Default: Print Mono (paper-like, low contrast).
  • Also available: Night Pro, Bamboo, Ceramic, Phantom, Pure Black.
  • Layout: width steps 30/50/70/100%, left or justify; line height & font size update immediately.

4. Fonts

  • Built-in set: LXGW WenKai, Noto Serif / Noto Sans, Source Serif 4, Literata, Noto Sans Mono, JetBrains Mono, IBM Plex Mono, FiraCode Nerd Font Mono (system stacks also available).

5. Highlights & Export

  • Select text to highlight or add notes; export to Markdown (download permission will be requested when needed).
  • Highlight colors follow the current theme for night/dark readability.

6. Privacy & Permissions

  • No data collection, no uploads.
  • Permissions only for injecting the reader and saving local settings; download prompts appear when exporting.
  • Settings live in local storage—remove the extension or clear browser data to reset.

Troubleshooting

  • Icon no response: refresh the page; ensure it’s not a browser special page.
  • Edge/Firefox “Failed to send toggle message”: switch back to the target tab then click, or reopen the tab.
  • Fonts/themes not changing: reselect in settings and save; some sites or special pages may block takeover.
  • Browser limitations: built-in pages (extensions, PDF viewer, etc.) cannot be overridden.
